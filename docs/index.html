TagUs App


The Peer to peer frictionless business facilitator



Created by Pedro Reis Colaço and Miguel Tadeu Mota


Why the name 'TagUs'?

Why the name 'TagUs'?

TagUs comes from Tagus (the river in Lisbon). Both developers of this project are from Lisbon and the Tagus river divides them since each lives on opposite margins.


The Problem

The Problem

Most of the times, product or service providers need to rely on third-party services (suppliers, transports, orders, etc) to have their job done.

In the end, the client needs to be satisfied, but also the provider needs to be happy.

A lot can happen along this line, like misunderstandings, payment delays and sometimes, even no payments at all.

QTUM blockchain is a great platform to bring some agility and peace of mind to this workflow.


The Solution

The Solution

TagUs makes your life a lot easier by making sure your business and your relations go smoothly, preventing abusive commercial relations and payment issues.

In the future, we want to enable funding for providers, so they can develop their projects, selling futures over their products.


What TagUs offers

What TagUs offers

Smart-contracts that guarantee smooth trading of products and services to everybody from the provider to the consumer

Exposure to the providers by displaying them on searches

Search engine connects local providers to local consumers

Search and notification facilities for everyone who wants to know about a service or product

Allow to make product or service reservations (secure the product until I get there or order it home)

Allow consumers to invest on new future product developments in the area


Workflow

Workflow

1. Create a Provider Profile

Here the provider inputs his details and location to be the default in any product or service published.

2. Publish an offer

The provider now publishes the product or service he/she is commiting to provide.

3. Consumers discover the offer

By searching or getting notifications setup for the product or service or categories, the consumer get awareness of the offers published.

4. Consumer purchases instantly or schedules a pickup or delivery

At this time the consumer already paid and the value is held on the smart contract until the finalization of the transaction. The workflow he is very interesting and minimizes the chances of bad behaviours. We welcome you to check the documentation of the github to know more about this flow...

5. Futures in the future

In the future we plan to build a similar workflow to allow providers to get funding for future productions through the same principles, enabling growth of businesses by crowd funding with the security of a trust-less escrow.

This enables a similar level of security of the major web retailers, but this time into the palm of the hands of any person in the planet independently of their location!

USE CASES


Use case 1

Use case 1

“Samantha is growing blackberries and has picked 15Kg today. She opens TagUs on the phone and announces that she has 15Kg of blackberries available to sell at a price. The phone gathers geo-location and account information and sends to the blockchain.
George is looking for blackberries for a desert and needs 1Kg. He opens TagUs on the phone and searches for blackberries in a 5Km radius. Samantha pops up and George checks the price. He thinks it’s a good deal and makes a reservation for 1Kg of Samantha’s blackberries. After a couple of hours, he reaches Samantha’s selling spot and concludes the transaction.”

Use case 2

Use case 2

“Margarida makes artistic birthday cakes, so she opens TagUs on her phone and instructs that to the application. Also that it should be ordered 2 days in advance and the price.
Patricia wants to celebrate her child’s birthday, so she looks in TagUs and finds that Margarida supplies cakes in her area. Patricia orders a cake several days in advance. At the day Patricia picks it up, the transaction is complete and the cake is automatically payed.”

Use case 3

Use case 3

“Samantha wants to change her product from blackberries to strawberries, but doesn’t know how the market will accept it and she needs investment to make this change. She opens TagUs and specify her intentions to grow 100Kg of strawberries next year and waits for investors.
Nathan has a local market in town and knows that strawberries are a good selling product, but scarce and pricy. He wants to do a good deal, so he opens TagUs and is happy to see that nearby, Samantha is willing to grow strawberries next year and since she’s getting investment for it, she’s willing to make reserves at a lower price. Nathan reserves 50Kg of strawberries for next year.”
